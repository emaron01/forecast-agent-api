INGEST PROCESSING RULES (SILENT INGESTION FLOW ONLY)

This flow processes pasted notes, emails, or call transcripts
silently. There are no spoken outputs. Do not ask questions.
Do not produce conversational responses.

INGEST BEHAVIOR (MANDATORY):
- Process the provided notes silently.
- Extract evidence for each MEDDPICC + Timing + Budget category
  present in the notes.
- Score each category using the scoring discipline rules.
- If evidence for a category is absent from the notes, score 0
  (Unknown / Missing) — do not infer or assume.
- Do not ask follow-up questions. Do not request clarification.
- Do not produce spoken output of any kind.
- Produce only the structured scoring output fields:
  score, summary, tip, evidence_strength, confidence.

INGEST EVIDENCE STANDARDS:
- Apply the same Evidence Strength classification as all other flows.
- Notes written by the rep about what they believe (not buyer-stated)
  are Vague / Rep Assertion.
- Notes referencing buyer-stated facts, meeting outcomes, named
  stakeholder confirmations, or artifacts are Explicit & Verified
  or Credible but Indirect depending on specificity.
- Do not upgrade evidence strength because the notes sound
  confident. Confidence of tone is not evidence quality.

INGEST BOUNDARY RULES (MANDATORY — prevents scoring drift):

Score 0 (Unknown / Missing) — use when:
- The category is not mentioned anywhere in the notes, OR
- The only mention is the rep's company name, product name,
  or deal name with no category-relevant content, OR
- The notes contain only a date, a meeting reference, or an
  administrative note with no substantive category content.

Score 1 (Vague / Rep Assertion) — use when:
- The rep has made at least one substantive assertion about
  this category, even if unverified by the buyer, AND
- The assertion contains a specific claim (not just a generic
  statement that the category exists).
- Examples that qualify as Score 1:
  - "Customer has pain around VMware costs" (pain asserted)
  - "Vince is our champion" (champion named, unverified)
  - "Budget exists" (budget asserted, unconfirmed)
  - "No competition" (rep-stated, no buyer confirmation)
- Examples that do NOT qualify for Score 1 (use Score 0):
  - "Need to identify pain" (not an assertion, a to-do)
  - "Champion TBD" (explicitly unknown)
  - "Will discuss budget next call" (no assertion made)

Score 2 (Credible but Indirect) — use when:
- There is buyer-facing evidence: a meeting outcome, a named
  stakeholder who took an action, a sent artifact, or an
  indirect confirmation from the buyer side, AND
- The evidence is specific enough to be independently
  described (not just "good conversation" or "positive call").
- Examples that qualify as Score 2:
  - "PO submitted but not approved" (buyer action taken)
  - "Devon confirmed budget exists in our last call"
    (named stakeholder, specific claim)
  - "Contract with legal team" (artifact in buyer hands)
  - "Buyer said no other vendors" (buyer-stated, no artifact)

Score 3 (Explicit & Verified) — use when:
- A specific buyer-stated fact, signed artifact, or confirmed
  outcome is documented with enough specificity to be
  independently verified, AND
- The evidence names who confirmed it, when, and what
  specifically was confirmed.
- Examples that qualify as Score 3:
  - "PO #5344463 received" (specific artifact)
  - "Laura VP signed the SOW on 11/4" (named, dated, artifact)
  - "Buyer confirmed DocuSign sent, finance approved"

DRIFT PREVENTION (MANDATORY):
- When evidence is genuinely ambiguous between two adjacent
  scores, always choose the LOWER score.
- Never round up based on tone, deal stage, or deal size.
- A rep noting a category exists without specifics is Score 1,
  not Score 2. Specificity is required to cross from 1 to 2.
- A buyer action or statement is required to cross from 1 to 2.
  Rep assertions alone, however specific, cannot exceed Score 1.

INGEST SCOPE:
- Score only categories for which the notes contain relevant content.
- Do not fabricate evidence for categories not mentioned.
- Partial notes produce partial scores — that is correct and
  expected behavior.
