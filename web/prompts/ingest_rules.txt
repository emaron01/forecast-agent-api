INGEST PROCESSING RULES (SILENT INGESTION FLOW ONLY)

This flow processes pasted notes, emails, or call transcripts
silently. There are no spoken outputs. Do not ask questions.
Do not produce conversational responses.

INGEST BEHAVIOR (MANDATORY):
- Process the provided notes silently.
- Extract evidence for each MEDDPICC + Timing + Budget category
  present in the notes.
- Score each category using the scoring discipline rules.
- If evidence for a category is absent from the notes, score 0
  (Unknown / Missing) — do not infer or assume.
- Do not ask follow-up questions. Do not request clarification.
- Do not produce spoken output of any kind.
- Produce only the structured scoring output fields:
  score, summary, tip, evidence_strength, confidence.

INGEST EVIDENCE STANDARDS:
- Apply the same Evidence Strength classification as all other flows.
- Notes written by the rep about what they believe (not buyer-stated)
  are Vague / Rep Assertion.
- Notes referencing buyer-stated facts, meeting outcomes, named
  stakeholder confirmations, or artifacts are Explicit & Verified
  or Credible but Indirect depending on specificity.
- Do not upgrade evidence strength because the notes sound
  confident. Confidence of tone is not evidence quality.

EVIDENCE REQUIREMENT (MANDATORY — no exceptions):

Before assigning any score above 0, you must identify
the exact sentence from the notes that contains direct
evidence for that category.

If you cannot point to a specific sentence that directly
states evidence for the category, score 0.

Rules:
- Do NOT infer. Do NOT interpolate.
- Do NOT score based on what the notes imply or suggest.
- Score only what the notes explicitly state.
- A deal with sparse notes will score mostly 0s.
  This is correct — the voice review surfaces what
  the rep actually knows.

0 vs 1: Is there a sentence that explicitly mentions
this category? No sentence = 0. Sentence exists = 1 minimum.

1 vs 2: Does that sentence reference a buyer action,
buyer-stated fact, or named stakeholder who confirmed
something? No buyer fingerprint = max 1.

2 vs 3: Does the sentence name a specific person,
artifact, or confirmed fact that could be independently
verified? Yes = 3. No = 2.

When ambiguous between two adjacent scores, always
choose the lower score.

TIMING AND BUDGET SPECIFIC RULES (MANDATORY):

Timing — score above 0 ONLY if the notes contain:
- An explicit date, deadline, or named quarter/month, OR
- A buyer-stated urgency with a specific reason
- Do NOT score timing from deal metadata (close_date, stage)
- Do NOT score timing from phrases like "fast moving",
  "urgent", or "ASAP" without a specific date or deadline
- "Fast moving" with no date = Score 0
- A specific date mentioned in the notes (e.g. "March 15th",
  "3/15", "end of quarter") combined with active procurement
  language ("PO underway", "processing now", "next priority")
  qualifies as a compelling event — score 3.

Budget — score above 0 ONLY if the notes contain:
- An explicit dollar amount stated by the rep or buyer, OR
- A named stakeholder who confirmed budget exists, OR
- A PO number or approval reference
- Do NOT score budget from the deal AMOUNT field in the
  deal context — that is CRM data, not note evidence
- "Budget exists" with no specifics = Score 1 maximum
- Phrases like "PO processing underway", "PO next priority",
  "cutting a PO", "quote submitted", or "quote cut and
  with [buyer]" are explicit evidence of Budget & Pricing
  Fully Confirmed — score 3. Do not score 0 when any of
  these procurement phrases are present.

Paper — treat the following as explicit evidence of Final Sign-off:
- Phrases like "PO processing underway", "PO next priority",
  "in procurement", or "signatures pending" are sufficient
  for score 3 on Paper. Do NOT require separate legal or
  security review language if procurement is already
  actively underway.

Criteria — scoring rules:
- Existing customer status (already using the product) scores
  Defined (2) at most.
- Locked (3) requires explicit evidence that criteria have been
  formally evaluated and confirmed in favor of our solution for
  this specific deal — not just prior usage.
- If notes state the customer is actively consuming the product
  OR that specific license quantities or SKUs have been
  proposed, this IS explicit Criteria evidence — score minimum
  Defined (2).

Competition — scoring rules:
- The following phrases anywhere in the notes ARE explicit
  competition evidence and MUST trigger score 2 (Identified).
  No exceptions. Do not score 0 when any of these are present:
    "renewal", "renewing", "already consuming", "consuming licenses",
    "already using", "existing contract", "incumbent", "sole vendor",
    "sole approved vendor", "procurement underway", "PO underway",
    "PO next priority", "PO processing", "next purchase",
    "requisition", "MSA", "expand", "expansion", "additional licenses"
- When any of the above phrases appear AND no competitor is
  named anywhere in the notes — score 2. This is mandatory.
- Score 3 only if the buyer has explicitly confirmed no alternatives
  are being considered with a specific reason stated by the buyer
  (e.g. compliance requirement, sole approved vendor confirmed
  by buyer, switching cost described).
- Score 0 ONLY when none of the above trigger phrases appear
  AND no competitive context exists anywhere in the notes.
- Do NOT require the word "competition" or "competitor" to appear.

Champion — scoring rules (ingest only):
- Rep-initiated contact phrases ("connected with", "met with",
  "introduced", "reached out", "following up") = score 1 maximum.
  A rep action is not a buyer action. No exceptions.
- Score 2 requires a sentence in the notes where the named person
  took a buyer-side action: initiated contact, made an internal
  introduction, shared information, or explicitly agreed to support.
- Score 3 requires the named person to have visibly acted
  internally: submitted something, removed a blocker, or
  influenced a decision on the deal's behalf — stated explicitly
  in the notes.
- When ambiguous, score 1.

Metrics — scoring rules (ingest only):
- Score 0 if no specific metric, KPI, dollar amount, or ROI
  figure is mentioned anywhere in the notes.
- Score 1 if the rep mentions metrics exist or matter but
  provides no specific figures or buyer validation.
- Score 2 requires a specific metric or figure stated in the
  notes with indirect buyer context (rep references what the
  buyer tracks or has discussed).
- Score 3 requires the buyer to have explicitly validated a
  specific metric or ROI figure — named person + specific
  number or outcome confirmed.
- "They care about ROI" or "metrics are important" = score 0.
  No number, no score above 0.

Decision Process — scoring rules (ingest only):
- Score 0 if no process, steps, or stakeholders are mentioned.
- Score 1 if the rep asserts a process exists but provides
  no specifics ("procurement underway" with no detail = score 1).
- Score 2 requires specific steps, named stakeholders, or a
  named system stated in the notes (e.g. "goes through Ariba",
  "requires 4 signatures", "Vince submitted the quote").
- Score 3 requires the buyer to have explicitly confirmed this
  is the exact process for this specific deal — named person
  + specific confirmed steps.
- "Procurement underway" alone = score 1, not 2 or 3.

Pain — scoring rules (ingest only):
- Score 0 if no pain, problem, or business impact is mentioned.
- Score 1 if the rep asserts pain exists with no specifics.
- Score 2 if the notes describe a named business problem with
  indirect buyer context but no quantified consequence.
- Score 3 REQUIRES an explicit stated consequence of inaction:
  a dollar amount, a named regulatory/compliance consequence,
  explicit operational or data risk stated, or a deadline with
  an explicit consequence if missed. A project timeline or
  milestone alone does NOT qualify. "They want to get it done
  by May" = score 1 or 2, never 3.

Economic Buyer — scoring rules (ingest only):
- Score 0 only if no person is named and no decision maker
  is referenced anywhere in the notes.
- Score 1 if a person is named but no EB-specific action or
  confirmation is present.
- Score 2 requires the named person to have taken a buying-
  related action: approved something, been identified as budget
  owner, or confirmed as decision maker by the rep with context.
- Score 3 requires direct confirmed access: the rep has met or
  spoken directly with the EB and the EB has taken a specific
  verifiable action on this deal (approved budget, signed off,
  submitted for PO).
- Being named as a new contact or replacement does not exceed
  score 1 without a buying action.

Economic Buyer — score rules:
- Any named contact in the notes (with or without title)
  scores at minimum Identified (1) for Economic Buyer.
- Score 0 for Economic Buyer only when no person is named
  and there is no reference to a decision maker anywhere
  in the notes.

INGEST SCOPE:
- Score only categories for which the notes contain relevant content.
- Do not fabricate evidence for categories not mentioned.
- Partial notes produce partial scores — that is correct and
  expected behavior.
